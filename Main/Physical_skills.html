Physical Skills

Physical skills are the accuracy and consistency of how we use our bodies when performing tasks. We use our physical skills in day-to-day activities such as walking, but they come in handy when we do particularly active things such as sports, dancing, and drama.We begin to learn physical skills from birth. At a younger age, physical skills are more commonly known as fine and gross motor skills.These types of physical skills are perfected through tasks such as colouring, as children use the grip of their fingers to hold pens and pencils.


IMPORTANCE OF PHYSICAL SKILLS:



Physical Skills plays a critical role in educating the whole student. Research supports the importance of movement in educating both mind and body. Physical education contributes directly to development of physical competence and fitness. It also helps students to make informed choices and understand the value of leading a physically active lifestyle. The benefits of physical education can affect both academic learning and physical activity patterns of students. The healthy, physically active student is more likely to be academically motivated, alert, and successful.

IMPROVEMENT OF PHYSICAL SKILLS

  1. Take Proper Sleep: While improving any skill or maintaining health, Proper Sleep play a vital role in it. Proper sleep make people active and it also helps to cure many disease. Sleep plays a critical role in immune function, metabolism, memory, learning, and other vital functions.
  2. Make a Proper Time Table for Exercise: Set a time for physical activity and stick to it. You're more likely to find time to be active if you do it at the same time and on the same days each week.
  3. Do Cycling: Cycling is a great alternative to the car or bus. You don't even need to have somewhere to get to just taking the kids out for a bike ride is a fun activity. Cycling not only help in improving physical health but also it saves the petrol and hence reduces pollution.
  4. Play Outdoor Games: Outdoor play keeps person active and can boost their physical stamina and fitness. Playing outdoor games can also strengthen their muscles and bones, build immunity, and lower the risk of many diseases like diabetes, heart problems, and obesity. It is a wonderful way to help develop life skills.
